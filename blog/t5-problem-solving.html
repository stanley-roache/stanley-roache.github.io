Problem Solving

Getting Blocked

I got quite blocked during the design process in Sprint 3. I was in the process of coming up with a new design for this website starting from base intention questions. I think in this case the issue was in two parts:

  • I felt like I had to get everything right and perfect the first time
  • I was fixating on the end result, not giving my full attention to each step along the way and breaking the process into manageable steps.

Looking ahead the task felt vague and unachievable. It was a bit stressful and unpleasant at first. My automatic response was something like "there's no time for this, I better force it until something gives to keep it moving". Obviously that's not so true or helpful, but it took a bit of effort to see that I was thinking that way and to change my approach.

The way I did it was first to stop trying to get immediate results and get some distance from the task at hand. Then I asked myself OK what am I actually trying to do here? I also voiced the issue I was having in one of the video checkins and got a few perspectives from others looking back at that sprint and some currently going through it.

Doing this gave me the space to see what I was expecting and how I was attempting to solve the problem through mental force. I came at it again with the intention of including just one or two features I knew I wanted and going through the design process step by step based around those aspects. This felt much more accessable and enjoyable.

An Elegant Solution

I found a puzzle on free code camp which was very satisfying to solve - building a function that converts numbers into roman numerals.

I think I relied heavily on pseudocoding here - to get a solution I had to break down the process into smaller and smaller steps until I could see a way to translate each step into code. There was no real getting stuck as such, just the process of taking a task that I could understand, making it general so it worked everytime, and breaking it down into pieces that I could code with.

Confidence in Techniques

I have a basic tendency to not acknowledge I'm stuck and try and put in a bit more raw effort in the hopes that the problem will just dissolve, doesn't work often! But once I do take a step back most of the techniques feel pretty natural.

The more attached I feel to the outcome, the harder it can be to take that first step back when I hit a wall.

Asking for help from peers or mentors can sometimes take a bit more, it very much depends on how willing I am to admit that I can't figure something the problem out, if I'm in a state of wanting to prove myself, how much trust I have that people are willing to help etc. Actually this is often difficult, I'd like to be able to more freely ask for help.

I think reflecting is a skill that I don't have good habits in using, I'd like to do this as part of my day more often, when I overcome something, have some strong experience or get stuck.

I'm looking forward to encountering more problems that require me to call on all of this.
