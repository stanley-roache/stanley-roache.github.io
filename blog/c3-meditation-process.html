Meditation Process

Process over Product

This approach was pretty difficult for me to get into and also liberating when I did.

As an example when I was going through the redesign process for the website based on the skeleton framework, I found myself gravitating around a certain look before I'd properly thought about the intention of my site.

  • Who is my target audience?
  • What is the purpose of the site?
I like the idea of stripping back all assumptions of how the end product should be and starting from the very basic questions, building up from there. In this case I did answer these questions but I kind of already had an image of how I wanted my site to look and behave, who knows, maybe letting this go and really focusing on those starting points of intention would have unlocked a more creative approach.

Remembering to engage in the process and let the product emerge definitely helped me to overcome getting stuck in details and perfection. I would remind myself that going through the redesign process I could trust that I'd get somewhere I liked even if it didn't fit some ideal and also that this was just the first iteration. I'd be looking at how my site appeared after a stint, coming up with new ideas and reworking it again and again.

Chapter Two: "Breathe as if your life depended on it"

I'm going to deviate from the suggested questions here. I guess none of what I am reading is conceptually new or unfamiliar to me, it is more a different framing and perspective which I am getting a lot from.

Past Practice - Forcing and Loss

I've had some experience of regular meditation and profound positive effects coming from it a couple of years ago. After a while I pushed it a bit hard and viewed it as a tool for self improvement, eventually I just couldn't very easily sit in meditation anymore, especially alone, without getting more and more tense and unhappy.

I like the analogy of a a person riding a horse in chapter one where horse and rider are different parts of a person. It was as though with meditation I had no respect for my horse, always whipping it to go faster until eventually it kicked me off (fair enough). I guess my attitude became very toxic, it was just another tool for betterment and I was never good enough to take it easy and appreciate things. This attitudinal part is the hardest part for me.

So reading about meditation and trying it out again is a little difficult. It has been rough to have and then lose that experience of enhaced state of self and regular practice, I'm unsure of my ability to get back on.

It's exciting to think I might be able to continue again. It would be great to approach mindfulness practice in a way that feels good and respectful and patient, I think doing it slowly and alongside other people would have a big impact in maintaining a good attitude.

I find it very ironic that I managed to make meditation, which is supposed to an act of allowing everything to be as it is, into a very stressful and forceful act for myself. If it can be done then it can be undone!

Second meditation experiment - 20min

This was great! I set myself a goal of 20min and to be honest I was expecting to get wound up in tension like I have the last few times I sat down and tried to focus on my breathe. I was surprised how calm and focused a state of mind I could get into. There was no mental forcing.

Perhaps reading SIY and having a good retrospect about what has tripped me up previously is allowing me to rengage with a more helpful mindset. I hope this is the case and I look forward to incorporating it into my day again.

Techniques I like

I really enjoy the 'easy' technique suggested by Meng. Using the breath as an attention anchor. It's easier for me to tell where my attention is this way when I do drift. I'm going to see how I go doing a short breathe focused meditation some mornings. I'm guessing that at some point I will get on the tension/forceful wind-up train during meditation as I get back into it, I'll have to be on watch for that and remember not to force anything, step back and maintain a gentle attitude.
