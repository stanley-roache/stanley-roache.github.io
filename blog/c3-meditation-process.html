Meditation Process

Process over Product

This approach was both pretty difficult for me to get into and also liberating when I did.

As an example when I was going through the redesign process for the website based on the skeleton framework, I found myself gravitating around a certain look before I'd properly thought about the intention of my site.

  • Who is my target audience?
  • What is the purpose of the site?
I like the idea of stripping back all assumptions of how the end product should be and starting from the very basic questions, building up from there. In this case I did answer these questions but I kind of already had an image of how I wanted my site to look and behave, who knows, maybe letting this go and really focusing on those starting points of intention would have unlocked a more creative approach.

Remembering to engage in the process and let the product emerge definitely helped me to overcome getting stuck in details and perfection. I would remind myself that going through the redesign process I could trust that I'd get somewhere I liked even if it didn't fit some ideal and also that this was just the first iteration. I'd be looking at how my site appeared after a stint, coming up with new ideas and reworking it again and again.

Chapter Two: Breathe as if your life depended on it

Past Practice - Strain and Loss

I've had some experience of regular meditation and profound positive effects coming from it a couple of years ago. After a while I pushed it a bit hard and viewed it as a tool for self improvement, eventually I just couldn't very easily sit in meditation anymore, especially alone, without getting more and more tense and unhappy.

I like the analogy of a a person riding a horse in chapter one where horse and rider are different parts of a person. I'll stretch that and say that when I was meditating regularly I felt as though I was riding a strong horse somewhere and it was a massive joy, I don't know exactly what part of me the horse was... Then I got impatient and began to whip the horse to go faster and not really respect it. It tolerated it for a while but eventually bucked me off and left. I guess my attitude became very toxic, meditation was just another tool for self-improvement and I was never good enough. This attitudinal part is the hardest part for me.

So reading about meditation and trying it out again is a little difficult. It has been rough to have and then lose that experience of enhaced state of self and regular practice, and now I'm unsure of my ability to get back on.

It's exciting to think I might be able to continue with that again. It would be great to approach mindfulness practice in a way that feels good and respectful and patient, I think doing it slowly and alongside other people would have a big impact in maintaining a good attitude.
